About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Optics at Palacký University Olomouc, Czechia. My research focuses on Quantum Optomechanics of levitated nanoparticles, exploring the fascinating intersection of quantum mechanics and macroscopic objects.

With a background in theoretical quantum optics from the University of Isfahan, my work aims to advance our understanding of quantum phenomena at the mesoscopic scale and develop novel applications in quantum sensing and quantum information processing.

Research Interests

My research explores quantum optomechanical systems with a focus on levitated nanoparticles. These systems provide a unique platform for studying quantum effects in mesoscopic objects and developing novel quantum technologies.

Quantum Optomechanics

Investigating quantum mechanical effects in optomechanical systems, particularly the quantum behavior of levitated nanoparticles in optical traps.

Quantum State Engineering

Developing protocols for creating and manipulating non-classical states of mechanical motion in levitated nanoparticles.

Quantum Sensing

Exploring the use of levitated optomechanical systems for precision sensing applications, including force and acceleration detection at the quantum limit.

Macroscopic Quantum Phenomena

Studying quantum superposition and entanglement in systems containing millions of atoms, probing the quantum-classical boundary.
